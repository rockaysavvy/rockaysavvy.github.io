References

This article is unfinished. It may contain incomplete or inaccurate information.

Rockay City contains a number of references to other works and media.

  • Skeety Kitty Arms Magazine features an article about Quentin Quarantino’s new movie “Bad Boy,” a reference to filmmaker Quentin Tarantino.
  • Computer Lab has several “thanks for the memory” posters, a reference to the song Thanks for the Memory from The Big Broadcast of 1938, which became a signature element of Bob Hope’s career.
  • In a cutscene, Baker compares Smith to James "Sonny" Crockett, an MDPD detective from Miami Vice. Later in the same scene, Baker says “Yippee-ki-yay, motherfucker,” borrowing John McClane’s catchphrase from Die Hard.
  • K-RCT Rockay City Talk Radio’s host Bickley St. James is named after sports radio host Dan Bickley and former Flashback host Bill St. James.
  • In Check Mate, the lines on the notepad in Wiz’s cell at Rockay Plains Correction Facility are from the 1842 version of Alfred, Lord Tennyson’s The Lady of Shalott.
  • Baker’s signature product has a distinctive blue color, a reference to Walter White’s blue methamphetamine in Breaking Bad.
  • The Urban Legend Secret Symphony is a reference to the 2001 film Ocean's Eleven.
  • The Urban Legend New Blood is a reference to the film Training Day.
  • The name of the mission Papers, Please is a reference to the indie game of the same name.
  • The SWAT spawns in the vents in the Dragon Dogs heist are inspired by the film Die Hard.
  • The slope at the beginning of Pinned Down is a reference to the first level in Tomb Raider III.
  • The magazine Fine is a reference to Time Magazine.
  • References’s appearance is a reference to Dennis Nedry from the film Jurassic Park.

Swiss

Damage135
RPM600
Range1000
Accuracy2.5
Magazine size8

The Swiss is an unlockable common pistol and a signature weapon of Axe, Cage, Midnight, and Slick, comparable to the Brescia 92. It is the most effective unlockable common weapon because of its superb reload speed, accuracy, and overall damage output.

Appearances

  • Axe, Cage, Midnight, and Slick all have Swisses.
  • Detective Phips uses a Swiss, except in story missions.
  • The jet pilot in Jet Set carries a Swiss.
  • Several police officers in Break Out use Swisses.
  • Several of the prison guards in Baller carry Swisses.
  • There are several Swisses in the RCPD weapon lockers in Break Out.
  • Ranger has a Swiss when he joins Baker’s crew in the campaign.
  • One of the prison guards in Baller drops a Swiss when he dies.
  • Elite security guards, police officers, elite police officers, and park rangers always wield Swisses, and tier I Chicos, tier I Graves, tier I Cagnali soldiers, Chico captains, tier I Riders, chief guards, security guards, and tier I SWAT officers can also wield them.
  • Tier I Chico snipers, tier I Rider snipers, tier I Scudo snipers, tier I Sicario snipers, and tier I SWAT snipers all carry Swisses.
  • Tier I SWAT shields always wield Swisses.
  • In turf wars, some tier I Chicos, tier I Cagnali soldiers, and tier I Riders have Swisses.
  • Tier I Scudo captains use Swisses, and tier I Chico captains can also wield them.
  • In character stories, East Men, Rider snipers, Scudo snipers, Sicario snipers, Viet Cong snipers, cops, prison guards, and park rangers use Swisses, and Chicos, Riders, Scudos, and Sicarios may also use them.
  • The Graves may find Swisses when they re-take a turf they have previously captured.
  • After players unlock the weapon in multiplayer, low respect and connected players can purchase Swisses from the black market in the campaign.
  • Common generic heisters who offer to join Baker’s crew in the campaign wield Swisses.

Trivia

  • The Swiss is based on the SIG Sauer P220.
  • The name “Swiss” comes from the English translation of SIG, “Swiss Industrial Company.”
